Current direction
GIG is being shaped as a musician ecosystem for recording, rehearsal, and live playback, with Music Tribe / Behringer as the first strategic review target. Keep the first-pass buyer story simple: product first, strategic fit second, AIP as deeper context only if the conversation opens.

Core product story
- Personal Workstation: each musician's endpoint for input, IEM mix, playback, talkback, patches, sheet music, and session tools.
- Cube: simple modular I/O path into GIG for fast setup without the full rack footprint.
- Racks: premium touchscreen-faced analog frames. The key new idea is swappable PCB analog cards for preamp, compressor, EQ, tone, dynamics, and future module families.
- Control: one command surface for rehearsal, studio recording, mix review, playback, and live operation. Lead with the 6-DOF SpaceMouse-style edit control.
- Venue: live playback and room translation layer. The important idea is mix-level reproduction: play the audio file at the recorded mix's level relationships, then translate that balance to the room.
